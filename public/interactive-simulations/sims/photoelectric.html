Photoelectric Effect

Light ejects electrons — only above a threshold frequency.

The Physics

Einstein showed light comes in photons of energy hf. An electron is ejected only if a single photon has more energy than the metal's work function.

KE_max = h f − φ

Increasing intensity ejects more electrons but doesn't change KE. Increasing frequency increases KE.
